What is going on?

Introduction Why did i choose this topic?

The latest report from EMCDDA shows a disturbing trend, after seeing an increase in about 50%, Sweden has the 2nd highest rate of people dying from opiate overdoses. With this presentation, i will show some statistics and present some insights from the data.

Total overdose deaths in Sweden, Finland and Portugal

To put things in perspective, the mortality rate in Sweden is twice as high as it is in finland, and I don't even know how small a percentage 33 people out of a population of 10 million is like in Portugal.

Healthcare vs. Legislation An unholy combination?

Sweden is pretty much the only country in the world where it is illegal to have narcotic substances in your bloodstream. Lots of countries have really odd ways of dealing with the legality of drugs and the treatment (or lack thereof) of addiction, like the current situation in the Philipines. The impact of this is that anybody who has consumed a narcotic can be arrested by suspicion alone, to later find your only option to be to pee in a cup or get your blood drawn. No distinction is made between substances, one is as bad as all of them. In the eyes of the law, an addict is criminal first, patient second.

Drug Offenders vs Drug Offences in Sweden

By comparing the total number of reported drug-related offences with the total number of drug-related offenders, we can see that a very small part of the population that are repeat offenders.

How did we get here?

Public drug health policy in Sweden has been in a very special place for a long time now. tracing the causes of this leads back to the moonshine days of the earlier centuries and the various systems set up to fight nationwide alcoholization, most notably Systembolaget, the policy has been based on moral opinions rather than medical science.

So with a legal framework that basically is standing in front of the hospital sticking it's leg out and tripping anyone trying to seek help. This creates a sort of catch-22 problem for many addicts, to get the medical help and treatment they need they must be completely clean from any drugs - but to be clean they need medical treatment. Addicts seeking help also risk getting into legal trouble since the highest priority is to make them pay for their crime rather than care for their health.
